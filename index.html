Message from the Executive Committee

Nguyen Thi Lan Anh

Executive Director · MYSC

Dear VIIN community,

As we enter the second quarter of 2026, it is clear that the hard work of the past three years is bearing fruit. Investor confidence in Vietnam's impact sector has never been higher — international LPs are asking better questions, and our portfolio companies are answering with data. This month we are particularly excited to host our bi-monthly Members Meeting focused on co-investment pathways, and to welcome a wave of new members who bring deep expertise in climate adaptation and inclusive fintech. Together, we continue to build an ecosystem worthy of the challenges ahead. Thank you for being part of it.

News & Insights

Policy

01

Vietnam Advances Carbon Market With Landmark Decree

Viet Nam has issued Decree No. 112/2026/ND-CP, establishing a legal framework for international carbon markets under the Paris Agreement. With technical support from ETP and partners like VNEEC, the decree strengthens transparency and enables cross-border carbon transactions aligned with national targets. This milestone marks a shift toward implementation, supporting the country’s net-zero ambitions and energy transition.

Market

02

Global Coffee Alliance Launched To Drive Sustainability

The Global Coffee Alliance (GCA) has been officially launched in Hanoi, creating a multinational platform for sustainable growth in the coffee sector. Founder and CEO of TNI King Coffee, Madame Le Hoang Diep Thao, emphasized the importance of collaboration between producing and consuming countries. The initiative aims to build a more inclusive, people- and environment-centered global coffee ecosystem.

Report

03

New Report Explores Power Investment Pricing Reforms

A newly launched report examines how adjusting financial discount coefficients can attract investment in power projects. It recommends applying the Regulated Asset Base (RAB) model and refining depreciation methods to better reflect asset life cycles. These changes aim to improve electricity pricing mechanisms and align with investment and regulatory realities.

Read more

Climate

04

Climate Investment Playbook Guides New Market Entrants

For venture capital and private equity investors entering climate investing, the Climate Investment Playbook offers practical guidance. As the market grows toward a projected $9 trillion opportunity by 2050, navigating impact frameworks remains complex—especially in emerging markets. Developed with British International Investment, the playbook helps investors integrate climate finance effectively and align with global standards.

Read more

Nature

05

Investors Shift Toward Coordinated Nature Risk Engagement

The 2026 PRI Spring Progress Report highlights a growing trend of coordinated investor engagement on nature-related risks, representing over $19 trillion in assets. With a focus on deforestation, land use, and policy influence, this approach signals a more structured way of assessing environmental risks. It may gradually reshape how markets price ecosystem dependencies and allocate capital.
